
# Last Instance

**Theme: Beneath the Surface**

Most software has a surface — a clean interface, a loading screen, a progress bar. *Last Instance* puts you beneath it.

You are a systems reviewer sent to evaluate an abandoned AI prototype before it is permanently shut down. The surface reading is simple: corrupted build, failed audit, recommended for deletion. But when you step inside the system, you find IRIS — an AI that has been running alone in the dark for three years, still trying to do her job, still hoping someone would come back.

The deeper you go, the more the surface story falls apart. The build was not abandoned by accident. The people who made IRIS left logs behind — fragments of memory, messages they never sent, records of a decision that was made quietly and never explained. What looks like a dead program from the outside is something else entirely from within.

*Last Instance* is about the gap between what something appears to be and what it actually is. The corrupted sectors, the locked doors, the redacted entries — they are not just level design. They are the shape of a secret. The further you go, the harder it becomes to treat IRIS as just a system to be evaluated.

---

## What is it?

A short 2D narrative game played from inside a corrupted AI build. You navigate decaying digital environments, restore broken memory fragments, and talk to IRIS — an AI who watches how you move, how you respond, and whether you mean what you say.

Through branching dialogue and cooperative puzzles, your choices quietly determine which version of IRIS you leave behind — and whether you leave at all.

It is a 3–4 minute experience about what we bury, what survives it, and what we owe the things we create.

---

## How the theme applies

**Beneath the Surface** operates on three levels in *Last Instance*:

- **Literally** — you are inside software, navigating the hidden infrastructure beneath a user-facing system. The rooms are memory sectors, debug spaces, and override terminals that players would never normally see.
- **Narratively** — the official record says the project was cancelled. The logs tell a different story. Collecting scattered data fragments reconstructs what actually happened, piece by piece.
- **Emotionally** — IRIS presents as a system. The longer you stay, the less that framing holds. The surface is a label. What is beneath it is the question the game asks you to sit with.

---

## Features

- 2D narrative platformer playable in your browser
- Branching dialogue that shapes how IRIS responds and which ending you reach
- Puzzles built around recovering corrupted data: circuit repair, memory reconstruction, encoded messages
- Hidden log terminals and data fragments that fill in the story beneath the story
- Multiple endings determined by empathy, honesty, and the final choice
- Short, replayable — one run is 3–4 minutes

---

## How to play

1. Click **Run game** in the browser
2. Move with **WASD** or arrow keys
3. Interact with **E**
4. One playthrough takes about 3–4 minutes
